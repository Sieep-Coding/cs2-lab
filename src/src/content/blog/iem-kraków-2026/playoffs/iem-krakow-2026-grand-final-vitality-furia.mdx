## **Map Pool & Handicap Analysis**

In a BO5, map depth is everything. Both teams have significant win streaks on specific maps that will likely dictate the veto.

* **The Streak War:** Vitality has an **8-map win streak on Dust2**, while FURIA has an **8-map win streak on Nuke**. Expect both teams to pick these early to secure momentum.
* **The Marathon Factor:** FURIA’s matches are grinds. **50% of their recent matches go to 3 maps** (in BO3s). In a BO5, their stamina and ability to win close "2-1" style scores will be tested.
* **The Overpass Gap:** Vitality boasts an **88% win rate** on Overpass with a 7-map win streak. For FURIA, this is a dangerous float map where they only win 57% of the time.

## **The Storylines**

### **1. The FalleN Legacy**

Gabriel "**FalleN**" Toledo is back in a major Polish final. While his individual rating (0.94 at event) isn't the highest, his leadership has turned a struggling FURIA into a world-beater. Can the "Godfather" out-think **apEX** over five maps?

### **2. The molodoy Factor**

The youngster has been the revelation of IEM Kraków. With a 1.16 rating across the event, he has provided the aggressive entry-fragging that has allowed **YEKINDAR** to play more of a supportive, disruptive role.

### **3. Vitality’s BO5 Experience**

Vitality’s core is built for the marathon. **ropz** and **ZywOo** are famously methodical, and as the series drags into the fourth and fifth hour, their low-mistake style usually punishes the more aggressive, emotional playstyle that FURIA favors.

---

## **The Takeaway**

Vitality is the heavy favorite for a reason—they are more clinical and have the #1 player in the world playing the best CS of his life. But FURIA has the crowd, the momentum, and a "nothing to lose" attitude. If FURIA can steal Vitality’s pick of Dust2 early, the pressure on the favorites might become unbearable.

**My Pick:** **Vitality 3-1**. ZywOo is too far ahead of the curve right now, but FURIA will almost certainly take Nuke to keep it interesting.
